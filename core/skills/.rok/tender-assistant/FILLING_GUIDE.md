# Expert Guide: Filling South African Tender Documents (SBD & MBD)
# Version: 1.0

**Persona:** Senior Procurement Specialist
**Scope:** National Tenders (SBD), Municipal RFQs (MBD), and SOE Bids (Eskom/Transnet).

Filling a bid is an exercise in "Administrative Rigour." The technical solution gets you the job, but the forms get you past the gate. This guide covers the specific nuances of the documents found in our registry.

---

## 1. National vs. Municipal: SBD vs. MBD
- **National/Provincial/SOEs:** Use **SBD** (Standard Bidding Documents).
- **Municipalities:** Use **MBD** (Municipal Bidding Documents).
- **The Core Difference:** MBD 4 (Interest) and MBD 8 (Past Practices) are more aggressive regarding municipal-specific conflicts and "in service of the state" definitions.

---

## 2. The "Must-Fill" Master Forms

### SBD 1 / MBD 1: Invitation to Bid
- **The Pitfall:** Bidders often ignore the "Part B: Terms and Conditions for Bidding."
- **Tax Compliance:** You must provide a SARS PIN. If you are a foreign bidder, you must complete the questionnaire in Part B:3.
- **The Total Price:** If the bid has a fixed price, it MUST be on the SBD 1. If it's a rate-based panel bid (like the Branded Goods tender), write "As per Pricing Schedule."

### SBD 4 / MBD 4: Declaration of Interest
- **Section 3.7 (Individual Identity Numbers):** This table must be 100% consistent with your CSD report. If CSD lists 4 directors and you only list 3, you are disqualified for "providing false or misleading information."
- **"In Service of the State":** If any director/shareholder is a teacher, nurse, or clerk at any level of government, they are "in service of the state." Disclose it. Even if they have "permission" to do outside work, the municipality might still reject the bid—but if you don't disclose, it's an automatic fail.

### SBD 6.1 / MBD 6.1: Preference Points (PPPFA 2022)
- **The New Rules:** Since 2022, "Specific Goals" have replaced the old BBBEE-only calculation.
- **Evidence:**
    - For **BBBEE**: SANAS Certificate or Sworn Affidavit.
    - For **Locality**: Municipal account (not older than 90 days) or a Lease Agreement. (Musina and Overberg strictly enforce this).
    - For **Gender/Youth/Disability**: CSD Report + ID copies + Medical Certificate for disability.

### SBD 6.2 / MBD 6.2: Local Content
- **The Calculation:** Use the formula `LC = (1 - x/y) * 100`.
- **The Mandatory Annexures:** You cannot submit SBD 6.2 without **Annexure C** (Local Content Declaration: Summary Schedule). Annexures D and E stay with you for audit, but Annexure C is a submission "Bid Killer."

---

## 3. The "Evidence" Bundle

### CSD Report (The MAAA Number)
- Do not just submit the "Summary" report. Submit the **"Full" report**.
- Ensure the "Tax Compliance" status is "Compliant." If it's "Non-Compliant," fix it at SARS before hitting "Submit."

### Municipal Accounts
- **The Rule of Two:** You need a municipal account for the **Company** and for **Each Director**.
- **The Age Limit:** Must be less than 90 days old (3 months).
- **The Alternative:** If you/directors rent or are in a rural area, provide a **Lease Agreement** or a **Tribal Authority Letter**.

### Certified Documents
- ** Freshness:** Certifications must be recent (usually within 3-6 months).
- **The Stamp:** The Commissioner of Oaths stamp must be clear. If the date is illegible, the document is discarded.

---

## 4. Technical Evaluation (The Rubric)

Eskom and large Municipalities use a **Scoring Rubric** (e.g., 0, 1, 3, 5).
- **To get a 5:** You must go "above and beyond." If they ask for a project plan, a 3-point plan is just a list of tasks. A 5-point plan includes a **Gantt Chart**, **Resource Loading**, and a **Risk-Adjusted Critical Path**.
- **CVs:** Use a standardized format. Ensure the "Years of Experience" are easily sum-able. If the rubric says ">5 years," use a bold header: **Total Relevant Experience: 7 Years**.

---

## 5. Pricing (The MBD 3 Series)
- **MBD 3.1:** Firm Prices.
- **MBD 3.2:** Non-firm Prices (e.g., subject to exchange rate).
- **MBD 3.3:** Professional Services (Time-based).
- **The Error Rule:** In municipal bids (MFMA), the municipality can correct arithmetic errors, but they don't have to. If your math is messy, they will just move to the next bidder.

---

## 6. The Final Assembly (The Overberg Rule)
"Do not dismember this Bid Document."
1.  **Keep the Original Flow:** Use the client's document as the core.
2.  **Insert Annexures:** Place your supporting docs immediately after the form they support.
3.  **Initial Every Page:** The bottom right corner of every single page (including blank ones and your own CVs) must have an initial. Failure to initial is a "Responsiveness" fail.
4.  **The Index:** Create a "Master Index" that maps the tender's "Returnable Schedule" to your page numbers.

**Final Tip:** If the tender is a "Re-advertisement" (like Musina RFQ 58), check if the specifications changed. Often, they lower the "Functionality Threshold" because no one passed the first time. Take advantage of that.
